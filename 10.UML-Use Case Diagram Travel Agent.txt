4. Detailed Relationship Explanations
Include (<<include>>):

Mandatory — the included use case ALWAYS executes
Example: "Book Flight" ALWAYS includes "Verify Customer" — you can't book without verification

Extend (<<extend>>):

Optional/Conditional — only happens under certain conditions
Example: "Cancel Ticket" EXTENDS with "Receive Refund" — refund only happens if cancellation conditions are met

Generalization (Inheritance):

"Is-a" relationship between actors or use cases
Example: "Pay by Cash" and "Pay by Card" are both types of "Make Payment"
They inherit the base behavior but add specific behavior


5. Viva Questions
Q1: What actors are in the Travel Agent System?

Customer, Travel Agent, Bank/Payment Gateway, Airline System, Hotel System.

Q2: Why is "Make Payment" included in "Book Flight"?

Because payment is mandatory for every flight booking — no booking without payment.

Q3: Why is "Receive Refund" an extend of "Cancel Ticket"?

Because refund is conditional — only given if the ticket qualifies for refund per policy.

Q4: What is generalization in use case diagrams?

When one actor or use case inherits from another. "Pay by Cash" and "Pay by Card" are generalizations of "Make Payment."

Q5: What is the difference between a primary and secondary actor?

Primary actor initiates the use case (Customer). Secondary actor is involved but doesn't initiate (Bank System).
